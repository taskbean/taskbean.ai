About

The bean has a thesis

Every productivity app wants your data on their servers. Every AI assistant needs an API key and a monthly bill. We think that's broken.

Why local-first matters

The AI revolution brought incredible tools — but it also brought a trade-off most people accepted without question: your data goes to someone else's computer.

Your task list is one of the most intimate data sets about your life. It knows what you're working on, what you're worried about, what you're procrastinating on, and what your deadlines are. Sending that to a cloud API for processing feels wrong.

Modern hardware changed the equation. NPUs, GPUs, and fast CPUs can run small language models locally with surprisingly good quality. Microsoft's Foundry Local SDK makes on-device inference accessible. The models are getting smaller, the hardware is getting faster, and the excuses for cloud-only AI are running out.

taskbean is a bet that the future of personal AI is local. Not because cloud AI isn't powerful — it is — but because not everything needs to be powerful. Sometimes you just need a bean that keeps track of your tasks and doesn't phone home.

What we believe

Privacy is a default, not a feature

Your data shouldn't leave your machine unless you explicitly choose to send it somewhere. No telemetry, no analytics, no "anonymized" data collection. Zero means zero.

AI shouldn't cost money to use

If the model runs on your own hardware, there's no marginal cost per request. No API keys, no token budgets, no "you've used 80% of your free tier" warnings. Use it as much as you want.

Open source builds trust

When we say "no data leaves your machine," you can verify it. Read the source. Audit the network calls. Fork it. MIT licensed — no tricks, no dual licensing, no "community edition" limitations.

Developers deserve better tools

Task management for developers shouldn't feel like a Jira ticket. It should be fast, keyboard-driven, AI-native, and built for how developers actually work — in terminals, editors, and browsers.

Built by

taskbean is built by Nicholas Brady, a developer who got tired of every productivity tool wanting his data, his money, or both.

The project lives under the @taskbean GitHub organization. Contributions are welcome — the bean doesn't care who writes the code, only that it ships.
